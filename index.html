Research Interests

Wearable RoboticsSoft RoboticsBiomechanicsControls

My research interest is to develop cutting edge technology in the domain of wearable robotics for functional movement assistance and/or remote monitoring, by using either traditional rigid robots or more hybrid solutions based on soft robotics. Wearable robotics is a real challenge but it is undeniably going to play a role in our future. Robots that can help and assist people, reduce injuries, improve recovery during rehabilitation, monitor body parameters and early diagnose potential diseases, compensate physical disability in an efficient way, or even "just" assist during fatiguing everyday tasks at home: make life easier!

There are several aspects of the human-robot interaction still to address (e.g. complex mechanical coupling with the human body) but I like to focus my efforts on:

  • how to smartly share the control of the body between the human user and the robot,
  • how to detect and anticipate the user intentions of movement,
  • how to adapt the robot initiative to the different applications and contexts,
  • how to collect meaningful data from the human-robot interaction.

As any engineer, I'm very interested in solutions that can have a real impact on people, translating out of the academic environment. Themes like cost-efficiency (for example through underactuation and minimal amount of sensors), robustness (validating our strategies under unsupervised behaviors by the user in unconstrained environments), proven usefulness (investigating the effects of a control strategy but also how to properly assess these effects) are all of interest in my daily activity.
